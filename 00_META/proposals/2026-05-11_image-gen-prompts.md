# Prompts para generadores de imagen — logo Mejía, IA & CIA

Cinco prompts distintos para que un generador de imagen (DALL·E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, Ideogram, ChatGPT con generación de imagen, Gemini, etc.) proponga un logo desde cero. Ninguno menciona M·IA·C ni sugiere una forma o letra concreta — la idea es que cada prompt invite una dirección creativa distinta y comparemos los cinco caminos.

**Cómo usarlos.** Copia un prompt completo, pégalo en el generador, deja que produzca 3–4 variaciones. Repite con el siguiente. Al final tendrás ~15–20 candidatos de cinco direcciones distintas para evaluar. Si un generador te pide "logo type" o "style preset", déjalo en blanco o usa "no preset" — el prompt ya carga la dirección.

**Importante.** Ninguno de los cinco menciona el acrónimo, las iniciales, ni "use the letter M". Eso es deliberado: queremos ver qué se le ocurre al modelo sin sesgarlo. Si un prompt da resultados tipográficos, los comparamos con los logos M·IA·C que ya tenemos; si da símbolos abstractos, ese es territorio nuevo.

---

## Contexto común (lo que cada prompt incluye de algún modo)

- **Nombre de la firma:** Mejía, IA & CIA
- **Sector:** consultoría de inteligencia artificial para pequeñas y medianas empresas (PyMEs)
- **Mercado:** América Latina con enfoque inicial en México y Colombia, vocación regional
- **Fundadores:** dos hermanos colombianos
- **Sede:** Tijuana, México
- **Propósito:** ayudar a empresas tradicionales latinoamericanas (familiares, de 10 a 200 empleados) a adoptar IA para automatizar procesos manuales — atención al cliente, ventas, reportería, análisis. No vendemos tecnología; vendemos resultados.
- **Personalidad de marca:** seria sin ser fría, técnica sin ser críptica, familiar sin ser informal, latinoamericana sin ser folclórica.
- **Cosas a evitar siempre (incluir en cada prompt como "negative"):** placas de circuito, cerebros estilizados, nodos de red neuronal, cabezas de robot, gradientes de neón, hexágonos genéricos, manos chocando, engranes, montañas, sol naciente, código binario, "tech swoosh" abstractos.

## Prompt 1 — Brief abierto, máxima libertad creativa

```
Design a logo for "Mejía, IA & CIA" — a Latin American consulting firm
founded by two brothers in Tijuana, Mexico. The firm helps traditional
small and medium-sized businesses (family-owned restaurants, distributors,
clinics, professional service practices across Mexico, Colombia, and
the broader Spanish-speaking market) adopt artificial intelligence to
automate manual work: customer service, sales follow-up, internal
reporting, data analysis. The firm sells outcomes, not technology.

Brand personality: serious but warm, technical but legible, modern but
durable. The kind of firm a sixty-year-old business owner would trust
with a year-long engagement, AND that a thirty-year-old CTO would
respect on first read. Think "boutique consultancy that happens to
specialize in AI" rather than "AI startup."

Take any creative direction that fits. Wordmark, symbol-and-wordmark,
monogram, abstract mark — your choice. Color palette is also open,
though the firm leans toward muted, considered colors rather than
saturated tech brights.

Format: clean vector-style logo on a neutral background. Show three
variations on a single canvas if possible.

Avoid: circuit-board lines, stylized brains, neural-network nodes,
robot heads, neon gradients, generic hexagons, handshakes, gears,
mountains, rising suns, binary code, abstract "tech swooshes." If you
catch yourself reaching for any of those, try a different angle.
```

## Prompt 2 — Casa consultora heredada que adopta IA

```
Imagine a Latin American consulting firm that has been around for
generations — the kind that occupies the top floor of a quiet building
in Mexico City or Bogotá, whose principals wear good shoes and never
chase trends. Now imagine that firm, in 2026, opens an AI practice run
by two brothers. The new practice serves small and medium businesses
across the Spanish-speaking world. Its job is to take traditional
companies — distributors, clinics, family manufacturers — and quietly
modernize them with artificial intelligence.

Design the visual identity for this practice. The firm is named "Mejía,
IA & CIA." The mark should feel like it was designed in 1962 and revised
once, in 2024, to admit the AI specialty — institutional, dignified,
type-led. Think Massimo Vignelli for IBM, or Paula Scher's most
restrained work. Editorial, not promotional. Confident, not loud.

A serif-based wordmark would be appropriate, but the brief is open. The
firm prints reports on heavy cream paper; the mark must look right at
the top of one.

Format: high-resolution vector-style logo, three variations on a single
canvas. Show one with the wordmark alone and one with an accompanying
small mark or detail, if you choose to include one.

Avoid: anything that signals "new," "startup," "tech," "AI." No
circuits, no neural nets, no swooshes. No futuristic typography. The
firm hates looking like a 2010s tech company.
```

## Prompt 3 — Identidad sistema, hecha en Suiza (minimalismo tech)

```
You are designing the visual system for an AI consulting firm called
"Mejía, IA & CIA," founded by two brothers in Tijuana, Mexico, serving
small and medium businesses across Latin America. The firm helps
traditional companies (10–200 employees, family-owned, often
second-generation) deploy AI to automate manual work and modernize
internal operations.

Approach this as a Swiss-school identity system. The logo is the
visible tip of a coherent system: a single, geometric, almost severe
mark; flat color; absolute precision; nothing decorative. The kind of
identity Linear, Vercel, Stripe, or a contemporary architectural
practice would commission. Pair the mark with a sans-serif wordmark
set with rigor — generous tracking, exact alignment.

The firm's clients are not in San Francisco. They are in Guadalajara,
Medellín, Querétaro, Cali. The identity should hold up there too —
serious enough to read as "firm," not "app."

Format: vector logo on a neutral light background. Three variations on
a single canvas. Show how the mark looks separated from the wordmark
(as a square avatar) versus locked up beside it.

Avoid: gradients, glow effects, isometric 3D, "AI"-cliché iconography
(circuits, brains, neurons, particles), and anything decorative. If a
design choice does not serve geometric clarity, remove it.
```

## Prompt 4 — Herencia visual latinoamericana, hecha a mano

```
Design a logo for "Mejía, IA & CIA," a consulting firm founded by two
Colombian brothers based in Tijuana, Mexico. The firm specializes in
helping Latin American small and medium businesses adopt artificial
intelligence to modernize their operations. Their clients are family
manufacturers in Bogotá, distributors in Monterrey, professional
practices in Lima — businesses with decades of history that are
crossing into the AI era.

Anchor the identity in Latin American visual heritage. Pull from the
geometric textile traditions of the Andes, from mid-century Mexican
graphic design (think Lance Wyman's pre-Olympic mark vocabulary), from
the colonial typography of Spanish-language books printed in Mexico
City and Bogotá in the 1950s. The mark should feel rooted — like it
belongs to this region, not imported from a San Francisco design
agency.

That said: it is still a 2026 brand for a contemporary consultancy.
The reference is heritage, not pastiche. Use the region's visual
language to ground something that is unmistakably current.

Color palette can lean warmer than typical tech: ochre, terracotta,
deep blue, cream — though that is suggestion, not constraint.

Format: vector-style logo, three variations on a single canvas. Show
the mark at large scale and at small scale (favicon size).

Avoid: clip-art sombreros, cactus, parrots, "Mexican-flavor" cliché.
Also avoid AI cliché (circuits, brains, neurons). The goal is dignity
and regional grounding, not decoration.
```

## Prompt 5 — Solo símbolo, sin texto

```
Design ONLY a symbol — no wordmark, no letters, no text of any kind.
A single iconic mark that can stand alone as the identity of a firm.

The firm is "Mejía, IA & CIA," an AI consulting practice founded by two
brothers in Tijuana, Mexico, serving family-owned small and medium
businesses across Latin America. The firm's deepest idea is bridging:
traditional businesses with decades of accumulated practice, on one
side, and contemporary artificial intelligence, on the other. Two
worlds, joined by a small firm that translates between them.

Capture that bridging quality in a single mark. It could be geometric,
it could be organic, it could be abstract or representational — but it
must be ONE mark, simple enough to live as a favicon, an avatar, an
embossed seal on a contract. No more than two or three elements.

Format: present the symbol on a light neutral background, then again
in a dark circular avatar context, then again at favicon size (32 by 32
pixels). Three variations on a single canvas if you want to show
alternative interpretations.

Avoid: anything literal about AI (no circuit boards, no neurons, no
brains, no robots, no chips). Anything literal about bridges (no
suspension cables, no archways). Anything literal about brothers (no
two figures, no handshakes). The goal is a mark that captures the
idea, not one that illustrates it.
```

## Después de generar

1. Junta los mejores 3–5 candidatos de cada prompt (15–25 totales).
2. Imprime cada uno a tamaño chico (favicon size) y tamaño grande (logo de carátula). Si pierde legibilidad en chico, descártalo.
3. Pásalos por la prueba "blanco y negro": un logo que solo funciona en color es frágil. El nuestro vivirá impreso, faxeado, fotocopiado por contadores que aún usan fax.
4. Compara los finalistas contra los 5 caminos M·IA·C del archivo `2026-05-11_logo-options.html`. La decisión final es entre los seis vectores (5 M·IA·C + el mejor "wildcard" de los generadores de imagen).
5. Una vez seleccionado, JP y Ricardo firman, y `build_brand_template.py` se actualiza para que el DOCX herede automáticamente la nueva marca.
